# Generative AI Models

**Role in AIG-NCII: Creation**

Contributes to the non-consensual creation of AI-generated intimate images.

**Description**

Generative AI Models refer to a spectrum of models that use deep learning techniques to take
in an input and produce a modified output. 

In the 2010s, smaller, independent, **open-source
models** primarily used [general adversarial networks](https://regmedia.co.uk/2019/10/08/deepfake_report.pdf) (GANs) to automate the face-swapping process. DeepFaceLab in particular had direct ties to Mr.DeepFakes, a [prolific](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11100) “deepfake pornography” [community](https://www.tsjournal.org/index.php/jots/article/view/126) and hosting site. Early models required a high level of technical expertise and were inconsistent in quality and realism. However, the images they produced, despite being less realistic, were still extremely [harmful](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0964663920947791) to victim-survivors depicted and publicly [documented](https://www.vice.com/en/article/reddit-fake-porn-app-daisy-ridley/) and criticized by researchers, activists, and journalists.  

With the 2020s came larger and more powerful generative AI models that produced realistic outputs. **Open-weight models** like Stable Diffusion and Flux have been fine-tuned to produce tens of thousands of fine-tuned [variants](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.03859) designed to produce AIG-NCII, predominantly of women. This trend is now being [replicated](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11815) with open-weight video-generation models. Additionally, proprietary **closed models** like xAI’s [Aurora](https://www.404media.co/groks-ai-csam-shitshow/), Google’s [Gemini](https://www.wired.com/story/google-and-openais-chatbots-can-strip-women-in-photos-down-to-bikinis/) models, and OpenAI’s [GPT](https://www.wired.com/story/google-and-openais-chatbots-can-strip-women-in-photos-down-to-bikinis/) models can be used via chatbot interface or API access to generate AIG-NCII.
